UPI Scams in India: How to Stay Safe

Published by Kavalkaran • Updated 2026

UPI has made digital payments easy in India, but it has also become a major target for cyber criminals. Every day, people lose money due to simple mistakes and lack of awareness.

Common UPI Scam Methods

1. Fake Payment Requests

Fraudsters send a “request” instead of payment and convince users to approve it, resulting in instant money loss.

2. QR Code Scams

Scammers ask users to scan QR codes claiming refunds or rewards. Scanning a QR code can NEVER give you money.

3. Fake Customer Support

Fake numbers on Google or social media pretend to be bank or app support teams.

⚠️ Never share OTP, UPI PIN, or approve unknown payment requests.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Only approve payments when YOU are paying
  • Never trust refund QR codes
  • Verify official support numbers
  • Enable app notifications
